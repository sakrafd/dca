DC Ambiance are accomplished musicians arranging and performing in the Django Reinhardt tradition. Their sound is described as a gentle and sophisticated approach to gypsy jazz. We are 2 guitars, concertina and rotating special guests.

Meet Us

Davorin (Buco) Cavar

Lead Guitar

Davorin Buco (sounds like Bootso, his childhood nickname) Cavar, a native of Sarajevo, began his career as lead with various rock bands in his hometown and in Chicago where he moved in 1995. Inspired by one of his lifelong musical idols Django Reinhardt since the early age, around 2004 he turned his attention to the acoustic guitar to focus on gypsy jazz. Buco has studied with such jazz guitar greats as Tony do Rosario and Alfonso Ponticelli. Upon moving to the DC area he helped form DC Ambiance, the newest addition to the gypsy jazz scene in the nationâ€™s capital.

Randy Stein

English Concertina

Randy Stein is a classically trained musician who studied the English Concertina with the famed Matusewitch Brothers, Boris and Sergei. A former circus acrobat and founding member of the Big Apple Circus, Randy performed in the US and Canada in festivals, shows, and theaters including recitals at Lincoln Center and Carnegie Hall. Randy has recorded with various musical artists including John Lennon and Yoko Ono on their album Double Fantasy. In addition to his love of gypsy jazz and performing with DCA, Randy arranges and plays a solo repertoire of Classic American and European Standards and is currently performing and recording with the Jewish Folk Band, Ein Lanu Z'man.

Bill Parmentier

Rhythm Guitar

Bill grew up playing rock guitar but was bit by the "Django bug" while living on Whidbey Island, WA (home of DjangoFest Northwest) in the early 2000s and befriending the late Dudley Hill of Pearl Django. During this time, he formed and performed with the Harbor Hot Club before moving to Northern Europe. Bill studied with famed gypsy jazz guitarists Robin Nolan and Lollo Meier. Bill extended his love of the genre by attending and jamming at some of the major gypsy jazz festivals in France and the Benelux region.
